Terminologies in Backtesting result:

1. Win Rate
The win rate indicates the percentage of deals you win out of all your trades.
2. Sharpe Ratio
A portfolio's returns less the risk-free rate of return per unit of standard deviation are used to compute the Sharpe ratio, which is the excess return. Utilizing the Sharpe Ratio, you may assess how well your strategy is compensating for the investment's inherent risk by contrasting your portfolio with a benchmark.
3. Maximum Drawdown
Maximum drawdown is a measurement of a portfolio's maximum loss from peak to trough during a given time frame. It is calculated as the price difference between the peak and the trough divided by the peak price. It is computed using percentages.
4. Annualized Returns
The annualised return is the geometric average of how much money an investment generates annually over a certain period of time.
5. Profit factor
The ratio of gross profit to gross loss
6. Buy and hold return
A passive investment technique known as "buy and hold" is purchasing stocks and holding them for an extended length of time without consideration to market movements.
